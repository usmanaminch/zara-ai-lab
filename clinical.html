Why medicine

I've wanted to be a dermatologist for as long as I can remember. Skin is the body's largest organ and the one most visible to the world — it affects how people see themselves and how others see them. I'm drawn to dermatology because it sits at the intersection of science, equity, and identity in a way few other specialties do.

Building AI tools around skin health has deepened that interest. Every project I've built — from a skincare ingredient checker to a medical case mystery game — has pushed me to understand the science behind what I'm building. My research papers on AI bias in dermatology and the skin microbiome came from the same curiosity. I want to be a doctor who understands the technology shaping her field.

I'm starting this journey early because medicine takes time to understand — and I want to understand it fully before I commit to it for life.
