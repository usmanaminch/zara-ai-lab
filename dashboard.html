What this data means

Dermatology is one of the most inaccessible specialties in American medicine. More than 60% of US counties have no dermatologist. Rural patients wait months longer than urban patients for the same appointments. Low-income and uninsured patients face the highest barriers of all. And when diagnosis is delayed — especially for skin cancer — outcomes get worse. These are not abstract statistics. They represent real people who couldn't get care in time. Understanding this gap is the first step toward closing it.
